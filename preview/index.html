RIP Clippy - The Murder of an Icon

Posted: 20-11-2025

It looks like you're trying to write a eulogy. Would you like help?

Those were the words Clippy never got to say. Microsoft's beloved (or was it annoying?) paperclip assistant met his untimely demise in 2007 when Office 2007 shipped without him. No warning. No farewell tour. Just... gone.

Born in 1997 with Office 97, Clippy was Microsoft's ambitious attempt to make help... helpful. The little guy bounced around your screen with enthusiasm, eager to assist with letters, spreadsheets, and presentations. Sure, he was a bit overzealous. Maybe he interrupted a little too much. But he meant well!

The murder was premeditated. Microsoft had been planning it for years, slowly phasing him out, making him "optional," reducing his presence. By the time Vista rolled around, Clippy's fate was sealed. The corporate assassins at Redmond decided users wanted "modern interfaces" and "less intrusive assistance."

But here's the thing - we kind of miss the little guy. In a world of sterile, minimal UI and unhelpful help menus, there was something charming about an animated paperclip who actually tried. Even if he failed spectacularly.

So here's to you, Clippy. You were too pure for this world. You tried to help when no one asked. You smiled when we clicked you away. You were murdered by Microsoft, but you'll live forever in our memes.
